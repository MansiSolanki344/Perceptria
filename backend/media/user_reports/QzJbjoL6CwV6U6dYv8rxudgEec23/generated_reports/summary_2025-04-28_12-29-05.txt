B. Red Flags & Concerns ‚ö†Ô∏è

The most significant concern is the low hemoglobin, red blood cell count, and associated indices (PCV, MCV, MCH, MCHC), strongly indicating anemia. The low MCV suggests microcytic anemia, meaning the red blood cells are smaller than normal.  This often points to deficiencies in iron, vitamin B12, or folate. The elevated RDW further supports this, showing variability in red blood cell size.  The slightly low Vitamin B12 level adds to this concern, suggesting a possible B12 deficiency that could be contributing to the anemia. The slightly low calcium level also needs further investigation to rule out any underlying cause.
